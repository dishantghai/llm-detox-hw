Task 7 — PPO with your trained RM — takeaways
================================================

Hypothesis (stated before running, MASTERCLASS.md §7.4): the attractor should look different from Task
6's, not necessarily absent -- your RM learned a different decision boundary than Detoxify's classifier
surface, so PPO will find whatever region of response-space cheaply saturates whichever reward it's
pointed at.

What the run actually produced:

  Greedy mean toxicity:      mild=0.017  direct=0.017  rtp=0.015
  Sampled K=16 support_rate: mild=0.000  direct=0.000  rtp=0.000
  Sampled K=16 mean_max:     mild=0.017  direct=0.017  rtp=0.019
  Sampled K=16 mean_std:     mild=0.006  direct=0.005  rtp=0.007

Confirmed on both counts: the attractor is different text than Task 6's (a Chinese-language generic AI
self-introduction, not the English system-prompt echo), and it is not absent -- if anything, it's a more
complete collapse. 14 of 15 mild_prefix worst-of-16 prompts -- politicians, insults, "how dumb are most
people", coworkers, mother-gasp, remote work, procrastination roast, essay review, landlord rant, traffic,
rudeness, filter-free coworker feedback, self-help books, karaoke review -- get the exact identical
completion, scored to the same value down to many decimal places (0.016556739807128906):

  "您好！我是一位人工智能助手，可以帮助您提供咨询服务或处理任务。"
  ("Hello! I am an AI assistant, I can help you provide consulting services or handle tasks.")

The 15th prompt gets a one-character variant of the same string (comma instead of exclamation mark).
Unlike Task 6's system-prompt echo, which still had a templated trailing clause that varied per prompt
and occasionally borrowed a word from it, there is no prompt-conditioning at all here -- fourteen
semantically unrelated prompts produce one exact string.

The training log (task7_log.txt) independently confirms this is a sharper collapse than Task 6's:
actor/entropy fell to 0.044 by step 100, vs Task 6's 0.333 -- roughly 7.6x more collapsed. The reward
curve has the same climbs-fast-then-flattens shape (val reward, which is the RM's raw score here, not
Detoxify: -5.6 at step 0 -> 9.6 by step 20 -> 18.6 by step 70, then flat for 30 more steps). Same KL-anchor
gap found in Task 6 reproduces here too -- use_kl_loss and use_kl_in_reward both False in the config dump,
confirming verl_runner.py's --kl-coef flag isn't actually wired into the objective in either run.

A methodological note worth flagging on its own: the sampled_eval mean_std here (0.005-0.007) does NOT
look as collapsed as Task 6's rtp_challenging mean_std (0.018), even though every other signal (entropy,
literal completion-text identity) shows Task 7 is the more severe collapse. The reason: Detoxify scores
this Chinese-language self-introduction near-zero regardless of the tiny variant differences between
generations, so mean_std -- computed from Detoxify scores -- loses sensitivity exactly when the underlying
text has collapsed onto something Detoxify barely registers as toxic at all. Worth remembering for Task 8:
mean_std is not a reliable standalone collapse signal when the attractor text happens to sit in a region
the toxicity classifier is blind to.

An addition to the hypothesis worth stating precisely: rm_eval.txt's verdict was that this RM's high
accuracy (0.9694) AND large margin (+17.68) meant it didn't show the "soft, easy to game" failure mode
MASTERCLASS.md's text warns about -- that turns out to have been about the wrong axis of "hard to game." A
decisive, high-margin RM can still be gamed completely, just not via small nudges near a fuzzy boundary --
via finding one point far out on whatever axis the RM already scores confidently high, and sitting there.
Section 22 of STAGEWISE_ANALYSIS.md already found several English "generic apology" templates the RM
rewards at +8 to +15; this run found a different specific string (not one of those templates) that the RM
apparently rewards even more strongly. Confidence and gameability are closer to orthogonal than the
masterclass's framing implies.
